The short answer: did much change?

Two kinds of change. Among delegations present at both meetings, most national positions held steady — so a side-by-side read feels stable. The real movement is (1) a handful of pointed position shifts among the regulars, and (2) a wave of new arrivals in May, including the entire skeptical bloc that was absent in March.
The cast changed — who showed up in May
The single biggest March→May difference is not delegations moving, but who took the floor. The negotiating blocs and the skeptical voices arrive in May.

How "movement" is classified. Only changes between two stated positions count as directional. Hardened = shifted toward skeptical/resist. Warmed = shifted toward supportive/welcome. Mixed = changes in both directions. Newly stated = stated a position on an axis it left silent in March (e.g. finance-link). Restated only = the only change is a stated position dropping to silent — not a substantive shift. Stable = no coded change.
Method & data isolation
  • Two isolated datasets. March positions come only from the 4 March readout; May positions only from the 7 May readout. Each was coded from its own transcript. This page never infers one meeting's position from the other.
  • Comparison, not bleed. The shared element is the coding schema (the four fault-line axes and bloc colours) — deliberately identical so the two meetings are comparable. The stance values themselves are kept separate.
  • Overlap = name match. A delegation counts as "present at both" only when the same country/group is coded in both readouts. Bloc-level May entries (G77/China, African Group, EU, AOSIS, CANS) have no single-country March equivalent and are treated as new entrants, not drift.
  • Inaudible exclusions. Saudi Arabia and China were inaudible in March (podium audio fault) — no March position on record, so they are excluded from drift. China's May position is therefore a new entry, not a measured shift.
  • Silent ≠ position. "Silent" means no explicit position recorded on that axis at that meeting. A silent→stated change is shown as drift only where the underlying statements support it.
